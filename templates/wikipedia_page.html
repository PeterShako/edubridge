    Wikipedia - The Free Encyclopedia

    Overview

    Wikipedia is a free, open-content online encyclopedia created through the collaborative efforts of a community of contributors. Written in multiple languages, it is the most comprehensive source of information available on the web today.

    History

    Wikipedia was launched on January 15, 2001, by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger. It started as a complement to the Nupedia project but soon surpassed it, becoming a global phenomenon with millions of articles contributed by volunteers worldwide.

    Over the years, Wikipedia has grown into a powerful educational resource, supported by the Wikimedia Foundation.

    Features

    • Free to use and access.
    • Available in over 300 languages.
    • Collaborative editing model allows anyone to contribute.
    • Comprehensive citations and references for credibility.
    • Powered by MediaWiki, an open-source software platform.

    The platform enables users to explore, create, and edit articles on an incredibly wide array of topics, making it a rich knowledge repository.

    References

    References and sources are essential to maintain the reliability and credibility of Wikipedia. Articles include citations to primary sources and trusted secondary sources.
